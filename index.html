01

Keeper Eligibility

  • 1.1
    A player must have at least 1 regular season at bat or 1 inning pitched to qualify to be kept.
  • 1.2
    Keeper salaries are based on ESPN's preseason suggested auction values at the time of the keeper lock date.
  • 1.3
    Managers may keep up to five players, not including rookie keepers (see Section 02).
02

Rookie Keepers

  • 2.1
    Managers may keep up to two rookies in addition to their five standard keepers.
  • 2.2
    A rookie is defined as a player who has played at least one MLB game and has not exceeded 130 at bats or 50 innings pitched in any single MLB season — exceeding either is disqualifying.
  • 2.3
    Rookie keepers are priced at 50% of ESPN suggested auction value, rounded down to the nearest dollar.
  • 2.4
    A player who exceeds the rookie threshold for the first time during a season may still be kept at the 50% rate the following season. After that, they must be kept at full value or released.
03

Auction Dollar Trading

  • 3.1
    Managers may trade auction dollars to one another. Transferred dollars take effect in the following preseason.
  • 3.2
    If a manager's keeper salaries total less than the dollars owed to them through trades, the full amount owed cannot be reflected and no further adjustment can be made.
    How this works: ESPN caps all managers at $260 in auction dollars. To reflect traded dollars, the commissioner deducts the equivalent value from a manager's keepers — effectively increasing their spending power.
04

Starting Pitcher Limit

  • 4.1
    No manager may exceed the league's weekly starting pitcher maximum in any matchup.
  • 4.2
    Managers are responsible for monitoring their own starts — ESPN does not prevent exceeding the limit in real time.
    Why this rule exists: ESPN's limit check does not occur until after the day's games. With 7 starters already logged in an 8-start max matchup, ESPN will allow additional pitchers to start on the final day — potentially pushing the total past the maximum. This rule closes that loophole.
  • 4.3
    If the limit is exceeded, the pitcher with the latest start time is removed from the starting lineup first. If two or more pitchers share the same start time, the one with the lowest ESPN % rostered is removed.
05

Playoffs

  • 5.1
    The top six teams qualify for playoffs, consisting of three 2-week matchups. The top two seeds receive first-round byes.
06

Entry & Payouts

  • 6.1
    The entry fee is $200 per manager, due before the auction draft.
  • 6.2
    Payouts are as follows:
    1st place—$1,200
    2nd place—$500
    3rd place—$300

08

Trade Ledger

01

Rules

  • 1.1
    Rounds 1, 2, and 3 draft picks may not be traded.
    Note: This is enforced by ESPN and cannot be overridden — attempting to include a top-3 round pick in a trade will be rejected by the platform.
02

Keepers

  • 2.1
    The league uses a 21-round snake draft. Each manager must designate three keepers before the keeper lock date — ESPN automatically assigns them to rounds 1, 2, and 3.
    Note: The commissioner will verify all keeper selections after the lock date. If a manager has not set their keepers by the lock date, the commissioner will assign them based on ADP (Average Draft Position).
  • 2.2
    Managers may optionally designate a fourth keeper if that player is in their second NFL season. This keeper occupies the round 4 pick.
    Note: The commissioner will verify second-year eligibility after the lock date.
03

Draft Lottery

  • 3.1
    Draft order is determined by a combination of dice lottery (picks 1–6) and playoff finish (picks 7–12).
    Picks 1–6—Dice lottery — see rule 3.2
    Pick 7—Most points among quarterfinal losers
    Pick 8—Fewest points among quarterfinal losers
    Pick 9—Winner of the 3rd place match
    Pick 10—Loser of the 3rd place match
    Pick 11—Runner-up (loser of the championship match)
    Pick 12—Champion (winner of the championship match)
  • 3.2
    Picks 1–6 are determined by a dice lottery using 20-sided dice (d20). Each non-playoff team accumulates dice over three consolation rounds — a win earns 4 dice, a loss earns 2 dice. The commissioner rolls each manager's accumulated dice and sums the result. The manager with the highest total receives pick 1, with remaining picks assigned from highest to lowest total.
    Tiebreaker: Total points scored across all three consolation rounds — the manager with more points receives the better pick.
